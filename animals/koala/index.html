Fascinating Koala Facts

ğŸ» Not Bears!

Koalas aren't bears at all - they're marsupials! Like kangaroos and wombats, they carry babies in a pouch. The "koala bear" nickname came from early European settlers who thought they looked bear-like.

ğŸ˜´ Sleep Champions

Koalas sleep 18-22 hours daily - more than almost any other mammal! This isn't laziness - eucalyptus leaves have so few calories that koalas must conserve energy by being inactive.

ğŸŒ¿ Picky Eaters

Koalas only eat eucalyptus leaves, and they're super picky! Of 700+ eucalyptus species, they only like about 50. Each koala has its own favorite trees and will reject others.

â˜ ï¸ Toxic Diet

Eucalyptus is toxic to most animals, but koalas have special bacteria in their gut to detoxify the leaves. Joeys eat special poop from mom (called "pap") to get these vital bacteria!

ğŸ–ï¸ Fingerprints

Koalas are one of the few animals besides primates with fingerprints - and they're so similar to human prints that they've occasionally confused crime scene investigators!

ğŸ‘ƒ No Water Needed

"Koala" is thought to come from an Aboriginal word meaning "no water" - they get almost all their moisture from eucalyptus leaves and rarely drink. During heat waves, they seek water.
