ü¶ç About Gorilla Trekking

Mountain gorilla trekking is bucket-list material for wildlife lovers. Daily permits are strictly limited (8 visitors per gorilla family), making early booking essential. The physical challenge varies - some families are found in 30 minutes, others require 4+ hours of hiking through dense forest. The one-hour visit is incredibly intimate - gorillas often approach within arm's reach. It's expensive but unforgettable.

Trekking Tips for Families

ü•æ Proper Footwear

Sturdy, waterproof hiking boots with ankle support are essential. Break them in before your trip! Many treks involve mud, steep slopes, and uneven terrain.

üí™ Build Fitness

Prepare with regular walks and stair climbing 2-3 months before challenging treks. Higher altitude (gorilla trekking) requires extra conditioning.

üéí Pack Light

Bring only essentials: water, rain jacket, camera, snacks. Many operators provide porters for heavy items. Less weight means easier hiking!

üß§ Wear Layers

Mountain conditions change quickly. Start with layers you can remove as you warm up. Waterproof outer layers are essential in rainforest environments.

üß¥ Bug Protection

Long sleeves, long pants tucked into socks, and insect repellent protect against tropical insects. Essential for lowland rainforest treks.

üì∑ Camera Ready

Keep cameras accessible in chest harnesses or easy-access pockets. Wildlife appears quickly - you don't want to miss the shot while digging through your pack!
