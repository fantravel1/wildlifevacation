Why Visit Masai Mara?

The Masai Mara is Africa's most celebrated wildlife reserveâ€”a place where dramatic river crossings, vast lion prides, and endless golden savannas create the quintessential safari experience.

What sets the Mara apart is its incredible predator density. With approximately 850 lions, one of Africa's highest cheetah populations, and excellent leopard sightings, this is where Africa's Big Cat Diary comes to life. The open terrain makes wildlife easy to spot, perfect for families and photographers alike.

Beyond wildlife, the Mara offers authentic cultural experiences with the Maasai people, who have coexisted with wildlife for centuries. Many camps include Maasai village visits, warrior dance performances, and bushcraft lessonsâ€”activities that kids love.

Common Questions

Masai Mara FAQs

When is the best time to see the Great Migration in Masai Mara? +

The wildebeest typically arrive in the Masai Mara from July and stay through October. The famous Mara River crossings usually happen between August and September, though exact timing varies each year based on rainfall patterns. For the best chance of witnessing crossings, plan a 5-7 day trip during this window.

Is Masai Mara safe for families with children? +

Yes, the Masai Mara is very family-friendly. Many camps welcome children of all ages and offer special family tents, kids' safari programs, and Maasai cultural experiences. The open savanna makes wildlife easy to spot, keeping children engaged. Game drives can be customized for shorter durations with young kids.

Should I stay in the reserve or a conservancy? +

Both have advantages. Conservancies (private land bordering the reserve) offer night drives, walking safaris, fewer vehicles, and off-road drivingâ€”plus they directly support Maasai communities. The main reserve has the Mara River for crossing viewing. Many families do a split stay: 2-3 nights in a conservancy for exclusive experiences, then 2 nights near the river during migration.

How much does a Masai Mara safari cost? +

Masai Mara safaris range from $350-$2,500+ per person per day. Budget camps start around $300-500/day. Mid-range camps run $500-900/day. Luxury camps and conservancy lodges range from $900-2,500/day. Prices increase significantly during migration season (July-October). A 4-day mid-range safari typically costs $2,000-3,500 per person including flights from Nairobi.
