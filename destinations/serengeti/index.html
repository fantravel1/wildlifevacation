Overview

Why Visit the Serengeti?

The Serengeti is quite simply one of the greatest wildlife destinations on Earth. Its endless plains host the world's largest terrestrial mammal migration‚Äîover 1.5 million wildebeest and 200,000 zebras moving in an eternal circle of life.

Beyond the migration, the Serengeti offers exceptional year-round game viewing. The resident wildlife includes some of Africa's densest lion populations, healthy leopard numbers, elephants, buffalo, and the rare black rhino. The diversity of habitats‚Äîfrom the iconic endless plains to riverine forests and rocky kopjes‚Äîsupports an incredible variety of species.

For families, the Serengeti offers an unmatched introduction to African wildlife. The animals here are accustomed to vehicles, allowing close, relaxed encounters perfect for children. Many lodges cater specifically to families with kids' programs, flexible schedules, and family-friendly accommodations.

Common Questions

Serengeti FAQs

When is the best time to visit Serengeti? +

The Serengeti offers excellent wildlife viewing year-round, but the best time depends on what you want to see. For the Great Migration river crossings, visit July-October. For calving season with baby animals, visit January-March. The dry season (June-October) offers the best general game viewing as animals concentrate around water sources.

Is the Serengeti good for families with children? +

Yes! The Serengeti is excellent for families. Many lodges welcome children and offer family rooms, kids' programs, and shorter game drives. Children typically love spotting lions, elephants, and zebras. We recommend children be at least 6 years old for the best experience, though some lodges accept younger children. The open plains make wildlife easy to spot, keeping kids engaged.

Can you see the Big Five in Serengeti? +

Yes, the Serengeti is one of the best places in Africa to see all Big Five (lion, elephant, buffalo, leopard, and rhino). Lions, elephants, and buffalo are commonly seen. Leopards are present but require patience‚ÄîSeronera is the best area. Black rhinos are rare but can be spotted in the Moru Kopjes area with luck and a dedicated search.

How much does a Serengeti safari cost? +

Serengeti safaris range from $300-$2,000+ per person per day depending on accommodation level. Budget camping safaris start around $250-400/day. Mid-range lodges run $400-800/day. Luxury camps and lodges range from $800-2,000+/day. Park fees ($70/adult, $20/child per day) are additional. A 5-day mid-range safari typically costs $2,500-4,000 per person including flights from Arusha.

How many days do you need in Serengeti? +

We recommend a minimum of 3 days in the Serengeti to experience the park properly, though 4-5 days is ideal. This allows time to explore different areas, maximize wildlife sightings, and avoid rushing. For the Great Migration, 4-5 days gives better chances of witnessing river crossings. Many visitors combine Serengeti with Ngorongoro Crater for a comprehensive Tanzania safari.
