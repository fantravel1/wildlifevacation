Tips for Families

ğŸŠ Snorkeling is Enough!

You don't need to dive to see amazing marine life. Most reef highlights are visible snorkeling in shallow water. Perfect for kids and non-certified adults!

ğŸ¦º Use Floatation

Life jackets and floatation noodles let kids (and nervous adults) relax and enjoy snorkeling without worry. Most operators provide them - ask in advance.

ğŸ§´ Reef-Safe Sunscreen

Regular sunscreen chemicals damage coral. Always use reef-safe formulas with zinc oxide or titanium dioxide. Many destinations now require them.

ğŸ‘“ Prescription Masks

Can't see without glasses? Prescription snorkel masks are available at most dive shops. Or use contact lenses with a regular mask.

ğŸ  Don't Touch!

Teach kids to look but never touch marine life or coral. Even gentle touches can harm delicate organisms. It's also safer - some creatures bite or sting!

ğŸ“¸ Underwater Cameras

Waterproof cameras or phone housings capture amazing memories. GoPros and similar action cameras are great for kids to use independently.
