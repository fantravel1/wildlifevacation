Why Tanzania?

Tanzania is widely regarded as the ultimate safari destination. No other country offers the combination of the Great Migration, the Ngorongoro Crater's wildlife density, pristine wilderness, and the tropical paradise of Zanzibar - all in one trip.

üèÜ The Safari Capital of Africa

Tanzania pioneered African wildlife conservation and remains the gold standard for safari experiences. The country protects over 38% of its land in national parks and reserves - more than any other African nation. From the endless plains of the Serengeti to the volcanic wonder of Ngorongoro, Tanzania delivers the authentic African safari experience that dreams are made of. For families seeking wildlife adventure, there's simply no better destination.

Best Times to Visit

‚òÄÔ∏è Dry Season (June - October)

The classic safari season with excellent game viewing as animals gather around shrinking water sources.

  • Best overall wildlife viewing
  • Migration river crossings (Jul-Oct)
  • Cooler temperatures, clear skies
  • Peak season - higher prices & crowds
  • Book 6-12 months in advance

üê£ Calving Season (January - February)

Witness thousands of wildebeest giving birth daily on the southern Serengeti plains - nature's nursery.

  • 500,000+ calves born in weeks
  • Predator action at its peak
  • Excellent photography conditions
  • Warm with occasional showers
  • Moderate crowds and pricing

üåßÔ∏è Green Season (March - May)

The "secret season" offers lush landscapes, newborn animals, and significant savings.

  • Dramatic 30-50% price reductions
  • Lush, green scenery
  • Excellent birdwatching
  • Afternoon rain showers
  • Some lodges close

üå§Ô∏è Short Dry (November - December)

Brief dry spell between rainy seasons - good value with improving conditions.

  • Migration returns to Serengeti
  • Lower prices than peak season
  • Fewer tourists
  • Good photography light
  • Unpredictable weather

üí° Our Family Recommendation

Best Overall: June-July or January-February for excellent wildlife, good weather, and slightly lower crowds than peak migration season. Best Value: November-December offers good conditions at lower prices. For River Crossings: August-September, but book at least 12 months ahead for lodges in the Northern Serengeti.

Practical Family Tips

‚úàÔ∏è Getting There

International flights arrive at Kilimanjaro (JRO) or Dar es Salaam (DAR). Charter flights connect to safari camps. Many itineraries start in Arusha, the safari capital. Allow for a night's rest before starting your safari.

üíä Health & Safety

Malaria prophylaxis is recommended for most areas. Yellow fever vaccination required if arriving from endemic countries. Tanzania is very safe for tourists with professional safari guides ensuring your security.

üë∂ Traveling with Kids

Most lodges welcome children 6+, some accept younger. Family rooms and inter-connecting tents available. Many camps offer junior ranger programs, shorter game drives, and child-friendly activities.

üí∞ Budget Planning

Tanzania safari costs range $350-2,500/person/day. Park fees are additional (~$70/adult/day). Mid-range safaris offer excellent value. Green season offers 30-50% savings with good wildlife.

üéí Packing Essentials

Neutral-colored clothing, layers for cool mornings, good binoculars, camera with zoom lens, sunscreen, insect repellent, and comfortable walking shoes. Soft bags preferred over hard cases.

üìã Visa & Entry

Most visitors need a visa - available on arrival ($50) or online in advance. Passport must be valid for 6+ months. No COVID restrictions currently in place.

Tanzania Safari FAQs

When is the best time to visit Tanzania for safari?+

Tanzania offers excellent safari experiences year-round. The dry season (June-October) provides the best general game viewing as animals congregate around water sources. January-February is ideal for witnessing the wildebeest calving season in the southern Serengeti. The Great Migration river crossings occur from July-October in the northern Serengeti.

Is Tanzania safe for family safaris?+

Yes, Tanzania is one of Africa's safest safari destinations with a stable government and well-established tourism infrastructure. Safari lodges and camps are very secure, and professional guides ensure safe wildlife viewing. Many lodges welcome families and offer child-friendly programs. We recommend children be at least 6 years old for the best safari experience.

Can you see all of the Big Five in Tanzania?+

Yes! Tanzania is one of the best countries in Africa to see all Big Five (lion, elephant, buffalo, leopard, and rhino). The Serengeti and Ngorongoro Crater are particularly excellent for Big Five sightings. Lions, elephants, and buffalo are commonly seen, leopards require patience, and the Ngorongoro Crater offers the best chances for black rhino.

How much does a Tanzania safari cost?+

Tanzania safaris typically range from $350-$2,500+ per person per day depending on accommodation level and season. Budget camping safaris start around $250-400/day. Mid-range lodges run $500-1,000/day. Luxury camps range from $1,000-2,500+/day. Park fees are additional ($70/adult/day in the Serengeti). A typical 7-day mid-range safari costs $4,000-7,000 per person.

How many days do you need for a Tanzania safari?+

We recommend a minimum of 7-10 days to experience Tanzania's highlights properly. A classic northern circuit covering Tarangire, Ngorongoro, and Serengeti needs at least 7 days. Add 3-4 days for Zanzibar beach time. For the Great Migration specifically, plan 4-5 days in the Serengeti to maximize chances of witnessing key events.
