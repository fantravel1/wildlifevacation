Whale Watching Tips for Families

ğŸ’Š Motion Sickness

The ocean can be rough! Take seasickness medication 30-60 minutes before departure. Natural remedies like ginger also help. Choose calmer, larger boats for young children.

ğŸ§¥ Dress Warmly

It's always colder on the water than land - even in summer. Bring layers, waterproof jackets, and hats. Many operators provide blankets but bring your own just in case.

ğŸ“· Camera Ready

Whales appear suddenly! Keep cameras accessible with wrist straps. Smartphone cameras work great. Don't spend the whole trip looking through a lens - enjoy the moment!

ğŸš¤ Choose Ethical Operators

Look for operators who maintain proper distances, limit viewing time, and follow local guidelines. Responsible whale watching doesn't chase or crowd animals.

â° Book Morning Trips

Morning trips often have calmer seas, better light for photos, and more active whales. Afternoon wind can make waters choppy - important if anyone gets seasick.

ğŸ’ Pack Snacks

Most trips are 2-4 hours. Bring water and light snacks. Avoid heavy meals before - a light breakfast is better for settling stomachs on choppy water.
