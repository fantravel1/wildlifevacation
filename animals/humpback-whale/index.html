Amazing Humpback Facts

ğŸµ Mysterious Songs

Male humpbacks sing complex songs lasting up to 30 minutes, repeated for hours. All males in a population sing the same song, which evolves over time. Scientists still don't fully understand why they sing!

ğŸ¦˜ Spectacular Breaches

Humpbacks launch their 40-ton bodies completely out of the water! They may breach to communicate, remove parasites, or just for fun. The splash can be heard for miles.

ğŸ«§ Bubble Net Feeding

Humpbacks work together to create "bubble nets" - spiraling circles of bubbles that trap fish. One whale then lunges through the concentrated prey. It's one of the few examples of coordinated hunting in whales.

âœˆï¸ Epic Migrations

Humpbacks make one of the longest migrations of any mammal - up to 16,000 km round trip! They feed in cold polar waters, then travel to warm tropical waters to breed and give birth.

ğŸ¦¸ Whale Heroes

Humpbacks have been observed protecting other species from orca attacks! They'll intervene to save seals, sea lions, and even other whale species. Scientists call this "altruistic behavior."

ğŸ‘‹ Unique Tails

Every humpback has a unique tail pattern - like a fingerprint! Scientists use tail photos to identify and track individual whales around the world.
