Fascinating Gorilla Facts

ğŸ§  Remarkable Intelligence

Gorillas use tools, solve problems, and express emotions like joy, grief, and empathy. They've been taught sign language and can learn hundreds of words. Each gorilla has unique fingerprints and nose prints!

ğŸ‘¨â€ğŸ‘©â€ğŸ‘§â€ğŸ‘¦ Family-Centered

Gorilla groups are led by a dominant "silverback" male who protects the family. Mothers are devoted parents, nursing young for 3-4 years. Siblings play together, and adults groom each other to strengthen bonds.

ğŸ¥¬ Gentle Vegetarians

Despite their strength, gorillas are peaceful herbivores eating leaves, shoots, and fruit. They spend about 30% of their day eating and can consume up to 40 pounds of vegetation daily!

ğŸ”ï¸ Mountain Dwellers

Mountain gorillas live at elevations of 8,000-13,000 feet in misty cloud forests. Their thick, long fur helps them survive cold temperatures that can drop near freezing at night.

ğŸ›ï¸ Nest Builders

Every evening, gorillas build fresh sleeping nests from vegetation - sometimes in trees, sometimes on the ground. They rarely sleep in the same nest twice! Even infants start practicing nest-building.

ğŸ’ª The Silverback

Adult males develop silver hair on their backs around age 12, earning the name "silverback." They're 10x stronger than humans and lead their families with mostly gentle authority, though they'll fiercely protect them if threatened.

âš ï¸ Important: Age Requirement

Mountain gorilla trekking requires visitors to be at least 15 years old. This protects gorillas from human diseases (they're susceptible to our illnesses) and ensures visitors can handle the physically demanding hikes through dense forest at high altitude. For families with younger children, consider Uganda's chimpanzee trekking (age 12+) or gorilla sanctuary visits instead.
