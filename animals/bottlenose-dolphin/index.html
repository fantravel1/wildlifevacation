Amazing Dolphin Facts

ğŸ§  Super Smart

Dolphins have the largest brain-to-body ratio of any animal after humans! They recognize themselves in mirrors, use tools (like sponges to protect their snouts while foraging), and can learn complex tricks.

ğŸ“¡ Echolocation

Dolphins "see" with sound! They emit clicks that bounce off objects and return, creating a detailed mental picture of their surroundings - even in murky water or complete darkness.

ğŸ˜´ Half-Asleep Swimming

Dolphins sleep with one eye open and half their brain awake! This lets them keep breathing (they must consciously come up for air) and watch for predators while resting.

ğŸ—£ï¸ Individual Names

Dolphins call each other by unique "signature whistles" - essentially names! They remember the whistles of dolphins they haven't seen for decades. They're one of the few animals known to use names.

ğŸ¤ Social Bonds

Dolphins live in tight social groups called pods. They help sick or injured members, teach their young cooperatively, and form alliances that can last a lifetime. They even grieve for the dead.

ğŸ¦ˆ Shark Defenders

Dolphins sometimes protect humans from sharks! There are documented cases of dolphins forming protective circles around swimmers when sharks approach - though scientists debate why.
