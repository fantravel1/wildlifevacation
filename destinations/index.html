Common Questions

Destination Planning FAQs

Expert answers to help you choose the perfect wildlife destination

What is the best wildlife destination for first-time safari travelers? +

For first-time safari travelers, we recommend South Africa's Kruger National Park or Tanzania's Serengeti. Both offer excellent infrastructure, reliable Big Five sightings, and options ranging from self-drive to luxury lodges.

Kruger is ideal if you want flexibility and self-drive options, with well-maintained roads and affordable rest camps. Kenya's Masai Mara is another excellent choice, especially during the Great Migration river crossings (July-October).

For families with children under 12, we particularly recommend South Africa due to lower malaria risk in many areas and family-friendly lodge policies.

When is the best time to visit Africa for a safari? +

The dry season (June-October) is generally best for game viewing as animals congregate around water sources and vegetation is thinner, making wildlife easier to spot.

However, the best time varies by specific destination and experience:

  • Great Migration (Serengeti): December-July in Tanzania, July-October in Kenya
  • Gorilla trekking: June-September and December-February (dry seasons)
  • Victoria Falls: February-May for highest water, September-December for activities
  • Birding: November-April when migratory species arrive

The green season (November-April) offers fewer crowds, lower prices, excellent birding, and dramatic photography with lush landscapes.

Are wildlife tours safe for families with children? +

Many wildlife destinations are excellent for families. The key is choosing the right destination and lodge for your children's ages.

Best family-friendly destinations:

  • South Africa: Excellent infrastructure, malaria-free options (Eastern Cape), and many family lodges
  • Costa Rica: Safe, diverse wildlife, great for all ages, no dangerous large animals
  • GalÃ¡pagos: Incredible wildlife encounters at any age, excellent for curious kids
  • Kangaroo Island, Australia: Self-drive friendly, diverse marsupials and marine life

Most safari lodges accept children 6+ on game drives. Some offer specialized family programs, children's guides, and shorter safari activities. We recommend destinations with diverse activities beyond game drives to keep children engaged.

How do you verify that wildlife destinations are ethical? +

We use a rigorous 47-point verification system that evaluates:

  • Conservation Impact: Direct contribution to wildlife protection, anti-poaching efforts, and habitat preservation
  • Community Benefit: Local employment, fair wages, community development programs
  • Animal Welfare: Ethical viewing distances, no baiting, no captive wildlife for tourist entertainment
  • Environmental Practices: Waste management, water usage, carbon footprint, renewable energy

Our team of wildlife biologists and conservation experts personally visit destinations. We cross-reference with organizations like IUCN, WWF, and local conservation authorities. Only destinations meeting our ethical standards are featured, and we regularly re-evaluate existing listings.

What is the best destination to see gorillas in the wild? +

Three countries offer mountain gorilla trekking, each with different advantages:

  • Rwanda (Volcanoes NP): Most accessible, best infrastructure, premium experience. Permits: $1,500. Easier terrain, excellent guides, combines well with other Rwanda attractions.
  • Uganda (Bwindi): More gorilla families, more affordable. Permits: $700. More challenging terrain but equally magical encounters. Better value option.
  • DR Congo (Virunga): Lowest permit prices ($400) but varying security conditions. Check current travel advisories. Best combined with Nyiragongo volcano trek.

All three countries share the same population of approximately 1,000+ mountain gorillas. For first-time gorilla trekkers, we recommend Rwanda for the smoothest experience or Uganda for better value.

Which destination is best for seeing the Big Five? +

Several destinations offer excellent Big Five (lion, leopard, elephant, rhino, buffalo) viewing:

  • South Africa's Kruger NP: Most reliable overall, excellent infrastructure, both self-drive and guided options
  • Tanzania's Ngorongoro Crater: Highest predator densityâ€”often see all Big Five in a single day within the crater
  • Kenya's Masai Mara: Excellent for lions and leopards, dramatic Migration crossings
  • Botswana's Okavango Delta: Exclusive water-based Big Five experience, premium lodges
  • South Africa's Sabi Sands: World's best leopard sightings, luxury private reserve

For guaranteed Big Five on a shorter trip, Ngorongoro Crater combined with Serengeti is hard to beat. For a more exclusive experience, Botswana's private concessions offer Big Five with far fewer vehicles.

How far in advance should I book a wildlife trip? +

Booking timelines vary significantly by destination and experience:

  • Gorilla permits: 6-12 months (Rwanda sells out quickly)
  • Antarctica expeditions: 12-18 months for best cabin selection
  • Great Migration camps: 9-12 months for peak season
  • GalÃ¡pagos cruises: 6-12 months for specific boats
  • Standard African safaris: 3-6 months for dry season
  • Costa Rica/general destinations: 1-3 months usually sufficient

Green season and shoulder periods offer more flexibility and last-minute deals. For bucket-list experiences, we recommend booking as far ahead as possible to secure your preferred dates and accommodations.

What destinations offer the best marine wildlife experiences? +

Top marine wildlife destinations:

  • Great Barrier Reef, Australia: World's largest coral reef, sea turtles, reef sharks, manta rays
  • GalÃ¡pagos, Ecuador: Sea lions, marine iguanas, penguins, hammerhead sharks
  • Tonga: Swim with humpback whale mothers and calves (Jul-Oct)
  • Baja California, Mexico: Gray whale calves, mobula rays, whale sharks
  • Maldives: Manta ray capital, whale sharks at South Ari Atoll
  • Raja Ampat, Indonesia: Highest marine biodiversity on Earth
  • South Africa (Sardine Run): Massive bait balls, dolphins, sharks, whales (Jun-Jul)

For non-divers, Tonga, Baja California, and the GalÃ¡pagos offer incredible snorkeling experiences with large marine life.
