Common Questions

Checklist FAQs

Everything you need to know about using our wildlife checklist

How do I use the wildlife checklist? +

Simply click on each species as you spot them during your wildlife adventure. Your progress is automatically saved in your browser, so you can continue tracking across multiple trips. The checklist works offline too, so you can use it even in remote safari locations. You can also print the checklist to take on safariâ€”just click the "Print Checklist" button.

Can I print the wildlife checklist? +

Yes! Click the "Print Checklist" button to generate a printer-friendly version. The printed version includes all species organized by category with checkboxes and space for notes about date, location, and behavior observed. We recommend printing on both sides to save paper. The checklist is formatted to fit standard letter or A4 paper.

What is the Big Five? +

The Big Five refers to the African Lion, African Elephant, Cape Buffalo, Leopard, and Rhinoceros (both Black and White). The term originally came from big-game hunting, referring to the five most difficult and dangerous animals to hunt on foot. Today, it represents the most sought-after animals to see on African safaris. We include both rhino species in our checklist since they're both incredibly special to encounter.

How rare is it to see all Big Five in one trip? +

Seeing all Big Five in a single safari trip is achievable but not guaranteed. In top destinations like Kruger National Park, Masai Mara, or Serengeti, experienced guides report that 70-80% of multi-day safaris (5+ days) successfully spot all five species. Leopards and rhinos are typically the hardest to find. Your best chances are during the dry season when animals congregate around water sources.

What wildlife can kids spot most easily? +

Children typically have great success spotting zebras, giraffes, elephants, hippos, warthogs, and various antelope species like impala. These animals are often abundant and visible during daytime hours when families are most active on safari. Large birds like ostriches, secretary birds, and eagles are also easy for kids to identify. Pro tip: Give kids their own binoculars and let them take turns calling out sightingsâ€”it makes the experience much more engaging!
