Unique Experiences

What makes Okavango Delta special for families

ğŸ›¶ Mokoro Safaris

Glide silently through papyrus-lined channels in a traditional dugout canoe (mokoro). Get incredibly close to hippos, elephants, and birds while your skilled poler guides you through the waterways. A magical experience kids will never forget!

ğŸ˜ Swimming Elephants

The Okavango is famous for elephants that swim across deep channels, sometimes with just their trunks above water like snorkels! The dry season (May-October) offers the best chances to witness this incredible behavior.

ğŸš Scenic Flights

A helicopter or light aircraft flight over the delta reveals the stunning maze of channels, islands, and wildlife from above. Spot elephant herds, hippo pools, and the unique patterns of this water wonderland.

ğŸš¶ Walking Safaris

Explore the delta's islands on foot with armed guides. Learn to track wildlife, identify plants, and connect with nature at ground level. Most camps offer family-friendly walks suitable for children 12+.

ğŸ•ï¸ Island Camping

For adventurous families, mobile camping safaris offer nights under the stars on remote delta islands. Fall asleep to hippo grunts and wake to lion roars - an authentic African wilderness experience.

ğŸ£ Fishing Safaris

The delta's channels teem with tigerfish, bream, and catfish. Catch-and-release fishing is available at many camps - a fun activity for families between game drives.

ğŸŒŠ The Annual Flood

The Okavango's waters originate as rain in Angola's highlands, taking 4-5 months to travel 1,000 km to the delta. This means the delta floods during Botswana's dry season (May-August), creating a unique phenomenon where peak wildlife viewing coincides with peak water levels. Animals concentrate on islands while the surrounding channels offer water-based safari opportunities found nowhere else on Earth.
