Why Costa Rica?

Costa Rica pioneered eco-tourism and remains the gold standard for sustainable wildlife travel. This small country packs incredible diversity - from misty cloud forests to tropical beaches - all easily accessible and wonderfully family-friendly.

ğŸŒŸ Eco-Tourism Pioneer

Costa Rica abolished its army in 1948 and invested in conservation instead. Today, over 25% of the country is protected land, and it generates nearly 100% of its electricity from renewable sources. When you visit Costa Rica, your tourism dollars directly support one of the world's most successful conservation models. This is eco-tourism done right!

Best Times to Visit

â˜€ï¸ Dry Season (December - April)

The most popular time to visit with sunny skies and minimal rain. Wildlife gathers around water sources, making animals easier to spot.

  • Best overall weather conditions
  • Peak whale watching (Dec-Mar)
  • Ideal for beach destinations
  • Higher prices and more crowds
  • Book accommodations well in advance

ğŸŒ§ï¸ Green Season (May - November)

The "secret season" - lush landscapes, afternoon showers, fewer tourists, and better prices. Mornings are usually sunny!

  • Dramatically lower prices (30-50% off)
  • Turtle nesting season (Jul-Oct)
  • Lush, green rainforests
  • Fewer crowds at popular spots
  • Brief afternoon rain showers

Top National Parks & Reserves

Costa Rica's park system protects incredible ecosystems from volcanic peaks to coral reefs. Here are the must-visit destinations for families.

ğŸ–ï¸ Manuel Antonio National Park

Costa Rica's most visited park combines beautiful beaches with excellent wildlife. White-faced capuchins, sloths, and iguanas are almost guaranteed. Perfect trails for all ages.

Best for: Families with young children, beach lovers

ğŸŒ‹ Arenal Volcano Area

Dramatic volcanic scenery, hanging bridges, hot springs, and abundant wildlife. Excellent infrastructure makes this region ideal for family adventures with all comfort levels.

Best for: Adventure seekers, hot springs, canopy tours

ğŸ¢ Tortuguero National Park

Accessible only by boat or plane, this "Amazon of Costa Rica" offers canal boat tours and the world's most important green sea turtle nesting beach (July-October).

Best for: Turtle nesting, boat safaris, remote adventure

â˜ï¸ Monteverde Cloud Forest

Mystical cloud forest draped in orchids and bromeliads. Home to the resplendent quetzal and incredible biodiversity. Famous hanging bridges and night tours thrill all ages.

Best for: Birdwatching, unique ecosystems, older kids

ğŸ¦œ Corcovado National Park

Called "the most biologically intense place on Earth" by National Geographic. Remote and wild - best for adventurous families with older children seeking pristine wilderness.

Best for: Serious wildlife seekers, teens, adventure

ğŸ’ Cahuita National Park

Practical Family Tips

ğŸš— Getting Around

Rent a 4x4 vehicle for flexibility - many roads are unpaved. Domestic flights save time between regions. Private shuttles are comfortable but pricier. Public buses work for the adventurous!

ğŸ’Š Health & Safety

No special vaccinations required. Tap water is safe in most areas. Costa Rica is very safe for tourists. Standard travel precautions apply - don't leave valuables visible in cars.

ğŸ’ Packing Essentials

Quick-dry clothing, rain jacket, good walking shoes, insect repellent, reef-safe sunscreen, binoculars for wildlife, and layers for cloud forests which can be cool.

ğŸ’° Budget Planning

Costa Rica isn't cheap but offers great value. Park fees are reasonable ($15-18). Tipping 10% is customary. Green season offers significant savings on accommodations.

ğŸ—£ï¸ Language

Spanish is official but English is widely spoken in tourist areas. Learn basic Spanish phrases - locals appreciate the effort! "Pura Vida" means everything from "hello" to "life is good."

ğŸ“± Connectivity

WiFi is widely available. Purchase a local SIM card for data on the go. Remote lodges may have limited connectivity - embrace the digital detox!
