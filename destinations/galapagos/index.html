Galapagos wildlife evolved without natural predators, so animals show no fear of humans. This means you can sit next to sea lions, walk past nesting birds, and snorkel alongside marine iguanas. There's nowhere else on Earth where wildlife encounters are this intimate and easy - perfect for families with children of all ages!

Family Travel Tips

ğŸš¢ Cruise vs Land-Based

Cruises access more islands but can cause seasickness. Land-based trips with day boats offer more flexibility for families and are often more budget-friendly.

ğŸ¤¿ Snorkeling for All

Even young children can snorkel in calm bays. Floatation vests are available, and sea lions often approach snorkelers curiously - magical moments!

ğŸ‘Ÿ Good Walking Shoes

Trails cross volcanic rock and sandy beaches. Closed-toe shoes with good grip are essential. Some trails are uneven - bring hiking sandals too.

ğŸŒ¡ï¸ Weather Varies

Warm wet season (Dec-May) has calmer seas and warmer water. Cool dry season (Jun-Nov) has more wildlife activity but rougher seas.

ğŸ’ Pack Light Layers

Mornings can be cool, afternoons hot. Quick-dry clothing works best. Bring reef-safe sunscreen and hats for sun protection.

ğŸ“‹ Limited Permits

Visitor numbers are strictly controlled to protect wildlife. Book well in advance, especially for peak season (Jun-Sep, Dec-Jan).
