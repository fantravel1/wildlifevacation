Overview

Why Visit Yellowstone?

Yellowstone is a place like no other on Earth‚Äîwhere the ground steams, geysers erupt on schedule, and wild bison roam across ancient volcanic landscapes. It's North America's premier wildlife destination and one of the last intact temperate ecosystems on the planet.

Home to the largest concentration of mammals in the lower 48 states, Yellowstone offers unparalleled opportunities to observe wildlife in their natural habitat. The reintroduction of wolves in 1995 restored the park's ecological balance and created what many consider the world's best wolf-watching destination.

For families, Yellowstone is magical. Children marvel at erupting geysers, spot bison from the car, and become Junior Rangers. The park's extensive road system makes wildlife viewing accessible to all ages and mobility levels, while boardwalks provide safe access to thermal features.

Family Fun

Family-Friendly Activities

Yellowstone offers incredible experiences for visitors of all ages

üåã

Old Faithful & Geyser Basins

Watch Old Faithful erupt every 90 minutes (approximately). Walk the boardwalks at Upper Geyser Basin to see dozens of thermal features. Kids love predicting eruption times!

üê∫

Wildlife Watching in Lamar Valley

Drive the scenic road through "America's Serengeti" at dawn or dusk. Bring binoculars to spot wolves, bears, and bison. Pull over at designated areas when you see wildlife.

üåà

Grand Prismatic Spring

The largest hot spring in the US and one of the most photographed features. Walk the boardwalk or hike to the overlook for the best views of the rainbow colors.

üèÖ

Junior Ranger Program

Pick up a free activity booklet at any visitor center. Kids complete activities, learn about wildlife and geology, and earn a Junior Ranger badge. Available for ages 4-12.

ü•æ

Easy Family Hikes

Try the Wraith Falls Trail (0.5 mi), Storm Point Trail (2.3 mi loop), or the boardwalk trails around Mammoth Hot Springs. Always carry bear spray on trails.

üåä

Yellowstone Lake & Canyon

Visit Artist Point for stunning views of the Grand Canyon of Yellowstone. Explore Yellowstone Lake's shores. Watch for ospreys, eagles, and pelicans.

Stay Safe

Wildlife Safety

Yellowstone's wildlife is wild‚Äîrespecting their space keeps everyone safe

‚ö†Ô∏è Critical Safety Rules

  • ‚Ä¢ Stay 100 yards (91m) from bears and wolves at all times. Use binoculars or telephoto lenses.
  • ‚Ä¢ Stay 25 yards (23m) from bison, elk, and other large animals. Bison injure more visitors than any other animal.
  • ‚Ä¢ Never approach, feed, or attempt to touch any wildlife. It's dangerous and illegal.
  • ‚Ä¢ Carry bear spray when hiking and know how to use it. Available for purchase/rent throughout the park.
  • ‚Ä¢ Stay on boardwalks near thermal features. The ground can be unstable and water is scalding hot.

If You Encounter a Bear

  • Do not run‚Äîback away slowly while facing the bear
  • Make yourself look large; speak in calm, low tones
  • Have bear spray ready (not in your backpack)
  • If charged, stand your ground‚Äîmost charges are bluffs
  • Use bear spray if the bear comes within 25 feet

Watching Wildlife Safely

  • Use pullouts and designated areas‚Äîdon't stop in the road
  • Stay in or near your vehicle when large animals are present
  • Bring binoculars and a telephoto lens for close views
  • Be patient‚Äîearly morning and evening are best for sightings
  • If an animal changes its behavior, you're too close
Conservation

Protecting Yellowstone's Wildlife

How the park preserves one of Earth's last great wildlife refuges

üê∫ The Wolf Reintroduction Success Story

When wolves were reintroduced to Yellowstone in 1995, it triggered one of the most remarkable ecological recoveries ever documented. With no predators for 70 years, elk had overgrazed riverbanks, causing erosion and habitat loss.

Wolves changed elk behavior‚Äîthey moved more and grazed less intensively. Willows and aspens recovered. Songbirds returned. Beaver populations increased. Even the rivers changed course as vegetation stabilized their banks. This "trophic cascade" showed how apex predators shape entire ecosystems.

Today, approximately 100 wolves live in Yellowstone, providing visitors one of the world's best opportunities to observe wild wolf behavior while maintaining a healthy, balanced ecosystem.

Practical Info

Planning Your Visit

Everything you need to know for a successful Yellowstone trip

Where to Stay

  • Inside the Park: 9 lodges and 12 campgrounds. Book 6-12 months in advance for summer. Old Faithful Inn is iconic; Lake Yellowstone Hotel is elegant.
  • For Wildlife Watching: Stay in Cooke City or Silver Gate (east of Lamar) or Gardiner/Mammoth area for early morning access.
  • Gateway Towns: West Yellowstone, Gardiner, Cooke City offer more lodging options. Jackson Hole is 60 miles south.
  • Winter Stays: Only Mammoth Hot Springs Hotel and Old Faithful Snow Lodge open in winter.

Getting There & Around

  • Nearest Airports: Bozeman (BZN) - 90 miles; Jackson Hole (JAC) - 60 miles; West Yellowstone (WYS) - seasonal.
  • Entrances: 5 entrances. North (Gardiner) open year-round. Others may close seasonally.
  • Getting Around: Personal vehicle recommended. The Grand Loop Road connects major areas. Gas available at several locations.
  • Winter Access: Most roads close Nov-April. Snowcoach and snowmobile tours available.

Common Questions

Yellowstone FAQs

When is the best time to visit Yellowstone for wildlife? +

The best time depends on what you want to see. May-June is excellent for baby animals (bison calves, elk calves, bear cubs). September-October offers elk rut and fall colors. Winter (December-March) provides the best wolf watching in Lamar Valley with fewer crowds. Summer (July-August) has the best weather but the largest crowds.

Is Yellowstone good for families with children? +

Yes! Yellowstone is one of the best family wildlife destinations in North America. Children love watching bison herds, seeing Old Faithful erupt, and exploring boardwalks at geothermal features. The Junior Ranger program engages kids of all ages. Many areas are accessible by car, making it easy for families with young children. Just be sure to follow wildlife safety rules.

Can you see wolves in Yellowstone? +

Yes! Yellowstone is one of the best places in the world to see wild wolves. Lamar Valley, known as "America's Serengeti," offers the best wolf watching, especially at dawn and dusk. Winter provides the highest success rates as wolves are more active and visible against snow. Bring binoculars or a spotting scope for the best experience‚Äîwolves are typically viewed from a distance.

How dangerous are the animals in Yellowstone? +

Yellowstone wildlife is wild and can be dangerous if approached. Stay at least 100 yards from bears and wolves, and 25 yards from bison and elk. More people are injured by bison than any other animal in the park‚Äîthey may look docile but can run 35 mph. Never approach, feed, or attempt to photograph animals up close. Use telephoto lenses and binoculars instead.

How many days do you need in Yellowstone? +

We recommend a minimum of 3-4 days to see Yellowstone's highlights, though 5-7 days is ideal for wildlife enthusiasts. This allows time to visit different regions, maximize wildlife sightings at dawn and dusk, and explore both geothermal features and wildlife areas without rushing. Many visitors combine Yellowstone with Grand Teton National Park (60 miles south).

Where should I stay in Yellowstone? +

For wildlife watching, stay near Lamar Valley (Mammoth Hot Springs, Cooke City, or Silver Gate). For geothermal features, Old Faithful area lodges are convenient. Canyon Village is centrally located. Book 6-12 months in advance for summer stays at in-park lodges. Gateway towns like West Yellowstone and Gardiner offer more lodging options and are often easier to book.
