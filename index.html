Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about planning your ethical wildlife adventure

The best time for an African safari depends on your destination. For East Africa (Kenya, Tanzania), the dry season from June to October offers excellent wildlife viewing as animals gather around water sources. The Great Migration in the Serengeti typically peaks from July to September. For Southern Africa (South Africa, Botswana, Zimbabwe), May to October provides optimal conditions with cooler temperatures and thinner vegetation making animals easier to spot.

Yes, many wildlife destinations are excellent for families! We recommend family-friendly lodges with age-appropriate activities, shorter game drives, and educational programs. Popular family destinations include South Africa (malaria-free reserves like Madikwe and Eastern Cape), Costa Rica, and the GalÃ¡pagos Islands. Most lodges welcome children 6+ on safari, with some offering special kids' programs including junior ranger activities and bush skills training.

Look for operators certified by recognized bodies like Rainforest Alliance, Travelife, or local ecotourism councils. Check if they support local conservation projects, employ local guides, limit group sizes, and follow responsible wildlife viewing guidelines. Avoid any operator offering direct contact with wild animals, walking with predators, or captive animal experiences marketed as sanctuaries. We only feature operators who meet our strict ethical standards.

The GalÃ¡pagos Islands are home to extraordinary endemic species including giant tortoises, marine iguanas, blue-footed boobies, GalÃ¡pagos penguins (the only penguins north of the equator!), sea lions, and Darwin's famous finches. You can also snorkel with hammerhead sharks, sea turtles, and rays. The islands offer year-round wildlife viewing, with different species more active during different seasonsâ€”sea lions give birth from August to November, while waved albatross are present from April to December.

The Big Five refers to Africa's most iconic safari animals: lion, leopard, elephant, rhinoceros (both black and white), and Cape buffalo. Originally a hunting term for the most dangerous animals to pursue on foot, it now represents the ultimate wildlife viewing checklist. You can see all five in several African countries including South Africa (Kruger National Park, Sabi Sands), Kenya (Masai Mara), Tanzania (Serengeti, Ngorongoro Crater), and Botswana (Okavango Delta, Chobe National Park). Each destination offers unique viewing experiences and different landscapes.
