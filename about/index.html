Our 50-Point Operator Evaluation Checklist (Preview)

Animal Welfare (15 points)

  • No captive breeding for tourism
  • Minimum approach distances
  • Time limits on encounters
  • No feeding wild animals
  • Stress monitoring protocols

Environmental Impact (15 points)

  • Waste management systems
  • Carbon offset programs
  • Water conservation
  • Renewable energy use
  • Habitat restoration work

Community Benefit (10 points)

  • Local employment %
  • Fair wages paid
  • Community ownership
  • Cultural respect
  • Education support

Conservation (10 points)

  • Direct conservation funding
  • Research partnerships
  • Anti-poaching support
  • Species monitoring
  • Habitat protection
Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Learn more about who we are and how we work

Who runs WildlifeVacation.com? +

WildlifeVacation.com is run by a team of wildlife biologists, marine scientists, professional safari guides, and conservation photographers with over 150 combined years of field experience across all seven continents. Our founder, Dr. Rebecca Nguyen, is a Stanford-trained wildlife biologist who previously worked as a Senior Scientist at WWF. Our team includes specialists in marine biology, primatology, ornithology, and family travel, all united by a passion for ethical wildlife tourism.

How do you verify that operators are ethical? +

We use a rigorous 50-point evaluation checklist covering animal welfare, environmental impact, community benefit, and conservation contribution. We personally visit operators, review certifications, interview staff, and monitor ongoing performance. Only operators meeting all criteria receive our verification badge. We also maintain ongoing relationships with operators and conduct periodic re-evaluations to ensure standards are maintained.

What conservation organizations do you partner with? +

We partner with WWF, IUCN, Wildlife Conservation Society, African Wildlife Foundation, Ocean Conservancy, Dian Fossey Gorilla Fund, BirdLife International, Sea Turtle Conservancy, and over 40 regional conservation organizations. These partnerships ensure our information is scientifically accurate and conservation-aligned. We also work directly with national park services and indigenous community organizations in many countries.

Do you accept sponsorships from tourism operators? +

No. We maintain complete editorial independence. We never accept payment for reviews or listings. Our recommendations are based solely on ethical and conservation criteria. This independence is fundamental to maintaining trust with our readers. We are funded through conservation grants, educational partnerships, and affiliate relationships that do not influence our editorial content.

How can I contribute to your conservation efforts? +

You can support conservation by booking with our verified ethical operators (a portion of proceeds goes to local conservation), donating to our partner organizations, participating in citizen science programs, or volunteering with conservation projects we recommend. You can also help by sharing our content, writing reviews of ethical operators you've visited, and choosing sustainable travel options whenever possible.

What certifications does WildlifeVacation.com hold? +

We hold certifications from Travelife, Green Tourism Business Scheme (Gold), and are members of the International Ecotourism Society. Our team includes certified wildlife guides (FGASA), PADI dive professionals, and accredited conservation educators. We are also recognized by the Global Sustainable Tourism Council and have won the Eco-Tourism Excellence Award and Responsible Travel Award.
