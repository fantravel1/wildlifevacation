Amazing Polar Bear Facts

â¬› Not Actually White!

Polar bear fur is actually transparent and hollow - it just looks white! Their skin underneath is black. The hollow hairs trap air for insulation and help them float when swimming.

ğŸŠ Marine Mammals

Despite being bears, polar bears are classified as marine mammals! They spend more time on sea ice and in water than on land. They can swim for days, covering 100+ km at a stretch.

ğŸ‘ƒ Super Sniffers

Polar bears have an incredible sense of smell - they can detect seals nearly a mile away and under 3 feet of ice! They wait patiently at breathing holes for seals to surface.

ğŸ¦­ Seal Specialists

Ringed seals are their main prey. Polar bears are so dependent on sea ice for hunting that climate change poses their biggest threat - less ice means less hunting ground.

ğŸ”¥ Built-In Heating

Polar bears are so well-insulated they can overheat! They have a 4-inch layer of blubber plus dense fur. They actually have to move slowly to avoid overheating while walking.

ğŸ‘¶ Tiny Cubs

Cubs are born in snow dens and weigh only 0.5 kg - about the size of a guinea pig! Mothers nurse them for 2+ years, teaching them to hunt on the ice.
