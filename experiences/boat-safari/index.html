Family Tips for Boat Safaris

ğŸ¦º Life Jackets First

All reputable operators provide life jackets - ensure children wear them at all times. Check that kid-sized jackets are available when booking. Safety briefings are mandatory.

âš“ Boat Stability

Mokoros require sitting still - assess if young children can manage this. Motorboats and larger vessels are more stable. Ask operators about boat type before booking with toddlers.

ğŸ¦› Close Encounters

You'll get remarkably close to wildlife from water! Brief children on staying quiet and seated. The reward is incredible photo opportunities and unforgettable memories.

ğŸ“¸ Photography Tips

Keep cameras secure with wrist straps - water and electronics don't mix! Shoot from a low angle at water level for dramatic shots. Polarizing filters reduce glare.

â˜€ï¸ Sun Protection

Water reflects sun intensely - apply waterproof sunscreen and bring hats. Choose boats with shade canopies for midday trips. Polarized sunglasses help spot underwater wildlife.

ğŸ¤« Stay Quiet

Silence is golden on boat safaris! The quieter you are, the closer wildlife will come. Make it a game - who can spot the most animals by staying silent?
